Theory

There are many different types of scales, but Major and Minor are the most common.

Here are the notes on a piano:

* Note that each sharp has a corresponding flat (and vice versa)

When working out the notes to play in a scale, the first note will be in the name of the scale - (so a Bb Major has a Bb)

Then we follow the interval formula to work out the next notes:

  • The Major scale formula is:
    W - W - H - W - W - W - H
  • The Minor scale formula is:
    W - H - W - W - H - W - W

Where 'W' / a whole tone is 2 semitones and 'H' / a half tone is 1 semitone

So, for example, to find the notes of a Bb Major Scale, we use the Major formula:

  • A whole tone from Bb is C
  • A whole tone from C is D
  • A half tone from D is Eb
  • A whole tone from Eb is F
  • A whole tone from F is G
  • A whole tone from G is A
  • A half tone from A is Bb (back to where we started)

Which means that the whole scale is: Bb C Eb F G A Bb

It is also important to know that there can only be one note for each natural / white key on the piano, this means that for the (theoretical) scale of B# Major, the notes are not: B# D E F G A B B#, however instead: B# C## D## E# F## G## A## B#.

(You could think of this as meaning that each note is one letter further in the alphabet from the previous, so for B# Maj the 'letters' are: B, C, D, E, F, G, A, B)
